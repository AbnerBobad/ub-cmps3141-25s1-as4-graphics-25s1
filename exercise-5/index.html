Exercise 5: Corporate Identity Design

Corporate Identity Designs

Business Card (3.5 x 2 inches)

Double-sided business card featuring the wify.me branding. The front displays employee information with clean typography, while the back showcases the logo with a bold brand color background.

Letterhead

Professional letterhead design with the wify.me logo positioned at the top, followed by contact information. The design maintains generous white space for content while keeping brand elements visible.

Envelope

Envelope design featuring the company logo, return address, and website URL. The design uses the brand's accent color for visual interest while maintaining a professional appearance.

Design Principles Applied

1. Repetition (Unifying Element)

I used repetition throughout all three designs to create visual unity:

  • Logo placement: The wify.me logo appears consistently in all designs, maintaining the same aspect ratio.
  • Color scheme: The three brand colors (#B3CC1F, #553F2E, #f7ecdf) are used consistently across all assets.
  • Typography: The Museo typeface is used throughout, with consistent font weights for headings and body text.
  • Accent bar: A lime green (#B3CC1F) accent element appears on the business card, letterhead, and envelope.

2. Contrast

Contrast was applied to create visual hierarchy and improve readability:

  • Dark brown text (#553F2E) against cream background (#f7ecdf) creates comfortable reading contrast.
  • The bright lime accent color (#B3CC1F) stands out against the muted brown and cream tones.
  • Bold logo placement contrasts with lighter text elements.

3. Proximity

Related information is grouped together:

  • On the business card, contact details (phone, email, website) are grouped together, separate from the name and title.
  • The company logo and URL are always placed in proximity to each other.
  • Address information is kept together as a single visual unit.

4. Alignment

Strong alignment creates a clean, professional look:

  • Text elements are left-aligned for easy scanning on the business card and letterhead.
  • The logo maintains consistent positioning across all designs.
  • Elements are aligned to an invisible grid for visual harmony.

Design Tools Used

Application: Figma

Process:

  1. Created artboards for each item (business card front/back, letterhead, envelope)
  2. Imported the logo.svg file and positioned it on each design
  3. Applied brand colors using the Figma color picker
  4. Added text using the Museo typeface with appropriate hierarchy
  5. Converted all text to outlines before exporting to preserve fonts
  6. Exported each design as SVG for maximum quality and scalability

Reflection

The design principles we learned in class were essential for creating a cohesive corporate identity. Repetition was especially important - by using the same colors, fonts, and visual elements across all three designs, I created a unified brand experience that would be immediately recognizable.

The 60-30-10 color rule helped me balance the use of colors: cream (#f7ecdf) as the dominant 60%, brown (#553F2E) for text and secondary elements at 30%, and lime (#B3CC1F) as the 10% accent for visual pop.
